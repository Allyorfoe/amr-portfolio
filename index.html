Engineering Work

Featured Projects

Two award-recognised engineering projects, each addressing a real clinical gap with precision-built solutions.

01
Capstone Project · Rehabilitation Engineering · Wearable Tech

PulsePlay —
Gamified Rehabilitation Glove

Oct 2024 – Jul 2025
Problem

Patients recovering from hand injuries or neurological conditions lack engaging, trackable tools for home-based rehabilitation. Conventional gloves offer no adaptive feedback, no measurable outcomes, and low adherence rates.

Engineering Approach

Developed a rhythm-based therapy glove with adjustable difficulty levels and a gamified interface designed for home use. Integrating sensor feedback with game mechanics transformed repetitive rehabilitation exercises into an engaging, data-driven experience.

Validated Results

Achieved a 40% improvement in finger mobility within just 2 weeks of testing, a clinically meaningful outcome that demonstrates the device's potential for accelerating functional recovery.

Recognition

Awarded Biomedical Best Project Award at the 8th Electrical Engineering Capstone Showcase 2025. Recognised for clinical relevance, engineering quality, and innovation value.

02
Final Year Project · Biomaterials · Tissue Engineering · Electrospinning

Electrospun PVA/Guar Gum
Nanofibers for Dermis Regeneration

Oct 2024 – Jul 2025
Problem

Wound dressings for dermal regeneration often cause secondary injury during removal, are poorly optimised for moisture management, and lack the precision drug-delivery architecture needed to support effective tissue healing.

Engineering Approach

Created electrospun nanofiber membranes specifically optimised for moisture retention and wound healing. Engineered scaffold architecture to support cell adhesion and dermal regeneration while eliminating painful removal, a critical driver of patient compliance.

Validated Results

Scaffolds achieved full dissolution in under 60 seconds, significantly enhancing dressing usability and eliminating the need for mechanical removal. Physicochemical characterisation confirmed optimal fibre morphology and moisture retention properties.

Recognition

Awarded 2nd Place, Best Overall FYP2 Project at the 7th International Innovation Day (Limitless 2025). Recognised for its translational clinical value and precision engineering methodology.
